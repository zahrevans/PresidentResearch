This is A Timeline of Jackson's Life
Scroll to Learn More



  1. 1767 Mar. 15

    Birth

    Andrew Jackson is born in Waxhaw, S.C., the third child of Andrew Jackson, Sr., and Elizabeth Hutchinson Jackson. Former linen weavers, Jackson's parents immigrated to the U.S. from northern Ireland in 1765, part of a larger Scotch-Irish trans-Atlantic migration. They established a small log-cabin homestead in Carolina mountain country near Twelve Mile Creek. Andrew Jackson, Sr., died shortly before the birth of his namesake son. Elizabeth Hutchinson Jackson, left a widow with the care of three young boys, moves to the nearby home of her sister and brother-in-law, the slaveholding farmers Jane and James Crawford.

  2. 1779-1781

    Start of Hero

    All three Jackson brothers serve in the American Revolutionary War. But his eldest brother dies in battle. Shortly after he and his other brother are both captured by the british,but after release his brother dies.

  3. 1781

    Elizabeth Hutchinson Jackson goes to Charleston to nurse the sick and dies of cholera or "prison fever." At age 14, Andrew Jackson is the only surviving member of his nuclear family of five.

  4. 1787

    Jackson is licensed as an attorney in North Carolina, where he practices law and tends store in Martinsville.

  5. 1797

    Political Start

    Jackson elected to the U.S. Senate by the Tennessee legislature. He takes his Senate seat November 22. and in 1798 he Takes leave of absence and then resigns his U.S. Senate seat.

  6. 1812

    The War of 1812 And Indian Wars

    The war of 1812 begins and Jackson is appointed as a General. Jackson leads troops to victory as well into victory in the Indian Wars aswell.

  7. July, 1822

    Pursuing the Presidency

    Andrew Jackson nominated as a candidate for the U.S. presidency by the Tennessee legislature.

  8. November, 1824

    Jackson runs in the presidential election as a Democrat-Republican. He wins a plurality of the popular and electoral vote over his leading opponent, John Quincy Adams of Massachusetts, but not the majority electoral vote necessary to be declared the victor. Under the terms of the 12th Amendment, the U.S. House of Representatives will decide which of the top three candidates will occupy the presidency.

  9. February 9, 1825

    First Loss

    U.S. House of Representatives selects Jackson's rival John Quincy Adams (1767-1848) as U.S. President. John C. Calhoun will be vice president. Jackson soon charges a "corrupt bargain" as Adams-backer Henry Clay is named Adams's choice as secretary of state.

  10. November 1828

    First Win

    Jackson elected the 7th President of the U.S. His presidential win, heralded as a victory for the common man, is dubbed "the Revolution of 1828." John C. Calhoun is Vice President.

  11. November 1832

    Jackson re-elected as President of the U.S., defeating Henry Clay.

  12. June 8, 1845

    Dies at the Hermitage, at the age of 78. His personal papers are in the hands of Andrew Jackson, Jr., at the Hermitage, and Amos Kendall, who has selected documentation for purposes of preparing a serial biography. And he is burried in the Hermitage Garden
